<p><ul><li>Banded Structure</li><li>Widmanstatten Structure</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hypo eutectoid steel, characterized by lower carbon content (typically below 0.76%), exhibits distinct microstructural types like banded and Widmanstätten structures. The banded structure features alternating layers of ferrite and pearlite, influencing mechanical properties like strength and ductility. In contrast, the Widmanstätten structure, formed during slow cooling, showcases elongated ferrite plates embedded in a pearlitic matrix. The market for these structures is integral to applications in automotive, construction, and manufacturing, where tailored mechanical properties are essential for performance and durability.</p></p>
