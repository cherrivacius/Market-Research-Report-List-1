<p><p>The Rigid Overhead Conductor-rail System (ROCS) is essential for powering electric vehicles, including trains and trams. The market is classified into two types: Hinged Type and Sliding Type. Hinged Type systems employ pivoted connections to allow for movement and flexibility, accommodating track curvature and dynamic loads. In contrast, Sliding Type systems use a continuous rail with sliding contact, providing stable energy transfer with minimal friction. Both types enhance efficiency and reliability in modern public transportation infrastructure.</p></p>
