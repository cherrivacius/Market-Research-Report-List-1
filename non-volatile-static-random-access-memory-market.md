<p><ul><li>Synchronize</li><li>Asynchronous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Non-Volatile Static Random Access Memory (NVSRAM) market can be categorized into two types: synchronous and asynchronous. Synchronous NVSRAM operates in sync with a clock signal, enhancing data transfer rates and efficiency, making it suitable for high-performance applications. In contrast, asynchronous NVSRAM does not rely on a clock, allowing for simpler designs and ease of use in low-speed applications. Both types serve diverse market needs, with synchronous options favored for speed-critical systems and asynchronous for cost-sensitive projects.</p></p>
