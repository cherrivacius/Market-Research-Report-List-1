<p><p>The railway Li-ion battery market primarily features two key types: LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) and Li-NMC (Lithium Nickel Manganese Cobalt). LFP batteries are known for their thermal stability, long cycle life, and safety, making them ideal for heavy-duty applications. In contrast, Li-NMC batteries offer a higher energy density, enabling longer ranges and faster charging, which is beneficial for high-speed trains. Both types are essential for enhancing efficiency and sustainability in railway systems, supporting the shift towards electrification.</p></p>
