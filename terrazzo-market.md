<p><p>The terrazzo market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic and epoxy terrazzo. Inorganic terrazzo, traditionally made with cement as a binder, features durable and versatile flooring that incorporates marble, glass, or stone chips, making it suitable for various applications. Epoxy terrazzo, on the other hand, utilizes an epoxy resin as the binding agent, allowing for more vibrant colors and intricate designs. Both types are prized for their durability, aesthetics, and low maintenance in commercial and residential settings.</p></p>
